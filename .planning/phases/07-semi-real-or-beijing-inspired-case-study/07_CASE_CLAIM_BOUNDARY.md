# Phase 7 Case Claim Boundary

**Phase:** 07 - Semi-Real or Beijing-Inspired Case Study
**Date:** 2026-06-16
**Status:** complete
**Case classification:** Beijing-inspired synthetic case

## Claim Scope

Phase 7 provides a bounded synthetic scenario-transfer check. It does not
provide real-world Beijing validation, real DRT operational evidence, or
semi-real evidence based on observed OD, road networks, stops, or fleet data.

## Allowed Wording

The following wording is allowed:

- "We further examine whether the qualitative patterns observed in the formal synthetic experiments remain visible in a Beijing-inspired synthetic scenario."
- "The case study should be interpreted as an illustrative scenario rather than direct evidence for real-world Beijing operations."
- "The case results provide bounded external-validity support, subject to the synthetic nature of the demand and network assumptions."
- "The Beijing-inspired synthetic scenario uses generated OD demand, a generated meeting-point grid, and simulated morning-peak request times."
- "The scenario-transfer result is illustrative and subordinate to the formal synthetic evidence."

## Forbidden Wording

The following wording is forbidden:

- "The proposed method is validated in real Beijing operations."
- "The Beijing case proves that the method will reduce citywide operating cost."
- "The case study establishes direct policy recommendations for Beijing operators."
- "The method is universally superior across real urban systems."
- "The Beijing scenario is calibrated to real Beijing OD or road-network data."
- "The case study proves deployment readiness for Chinese city operators."

## Phase 8 Use

Phase 7 may be used in Phase 8 only as **limitation-level** or, at most,
**exploratory scenario-transfer** evidence. It must not support strong or
moderate claims.

Permitted Phase 8 use:

- Note that an existing Beijing-inspired synthetic scenario was audited.
- State that current case evidence is synthetic and externally limited.
- Use the legacy rows as a directional illustration only if Phase 8 chooses to
  mention them.
- Reinforce the limitation that real/semi-real case evidence is absent.

Forbidden Phase 8 use:

- Upgrading any case-study finding to a strong or moderate manuscript claim.
- Treating the Beijing-inspired result as real city evidence.
- Using the case result to make policy prescriptions.
- Using the case result to override Phase 6 formal synthetic limitations.

## Abstract Boundary

The Phase 7 case does not justify a strong abstract claim. The abstract should
not present Beijing results as a main validation finding. If mentioned at all
after Phase 8, it should be brief and explicitly synthetic, for example:

"A Beijing-inspired synthetic scenario is used as an illustrative transfer
check, while real-city validation remains future work."

## Manuscript Placement

The case material belongs in discussion, limitations, or an appendix/supplement
unless Phase 8 explicitly promotes a bounded sentence. It should not drive the
paper's headline result, conclusion, or policy claims.

## Remaining Limitations

- No observed OD demand.
- No real road network.
- No transit-stop, POI, or road-node meeting-point candidates.
- No observed request-time distribution.
- No real passenger preference calibration.
- No observed fleet setting.
- No Phase 7 formal raw/processed/config/validation package.
