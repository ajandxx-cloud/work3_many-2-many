# Phase 1 Revised Research Questions

**Phase:** 01 - Literature and Novelty Audit
**Plan:** 01-01
**Date:** 2026-06-15
**Status:** complete

## Revised Research Questions

1. Under the Phase 2 fair-comparison contract and Phase 6 paired-seed formal experiments, when does bidirectional pickup/dropoff meeting-point service design reduce vehicle-kilometres per served trip relative to door-to-door and single-sided variants while holding passenger-response assumptions constant?

2. Under the Phase 2 matched-coverage and fixed accepted-set designs, how much of the observed efficiency difference is attributable to route consolidation rather than lower served share or passenger rejection?

3. Under the Phase 3 passenger-choice rebuild and Phase 6 sensitivity experiments, how do walk, wait, in-vehicle time, fare, outside-option, and passenger-type parameters change acceptance, served share, and generalized passenger burden?

4. Under the Phase 4 algorithm audit and Phase 6 formal experiments, what level of routing quality and runtime evidence is sufficient to present rolling-horizon ALNS as a scalable heuristic rather than an exact benchmark?

5. Under the Phase 7 case-study decision and Phase 8 claim gate, which managerial or policy insights remain defensible as simulation-based findings, and which require real or semi-real data before manuscript use in Phase 9?

## Claim Discipline

These questions are conditional and testable. They avoid universal superiority language and defer final claim strength to Phase 8. They also separate the following evidence channels:

- Phase 2: method taxonomy, fair comparison contract, metric denominators, matched coverage, fixed accepted sets.
- Phase 3: passenger utility and sensitivity assumptions.
- Phase 4: algorithm and exact-diagnostic scope.
- Phase 6: formal paired-seed synthetic evidence.
- Phase 7: case-study external validity.
- Phase 8: final claim classification.
- Phase 9: manuscript restructuring.
