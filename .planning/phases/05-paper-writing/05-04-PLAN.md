\subsection{R1: Set Walking Radius Threshold at 1000\,m}
~150 words. Evidence: ρ=1000m is the minimum viable threshold (acceptance 9.0% vs 0.0% at ρ≤400m). Policy: survey walking tolerance before deployment; districts with elderly populations or poor pedestrian infrastructure should retain door-to-door. Cite FullModel vkm=219.7 vs DoorToDoor=1693.8 at ρ=1000m.

\subsection{R2: Minimum Fleet Ratio of 15 Vehicles per 100 Daily Requests}
~150 words. Evidence: FullModel acceptance peaks at 24.0% with n_vehicles=30 (15 per 100 requests). DoorToDoor exceeds FullModel by 38 pp at n_vehicles=15. Policy: budget for ≥15 vehicles per 100 peak-hour requests; phase in vehicles using the sensitivity curve.

\subsection{R3: Monitor Time-Sensitive Passengers for Service Equity}
~200 words. Evidence: time-sensitive acceptance 14.4% vs price-sensitive 25.4%; Gini=0.122. Policy: track per-type acceptance rates; consider door-to-door fallback or fare subsidies for disadvantaged types. Regulatory recommendation: require disaggregated acceptance rate reporting as condition of operating license.

\subsection{R4: Match Service Mode to City Density Tier}
~200 words. Evidence: FullModel vkm=628.5 vs DoorToDoor=2603.7 (75.9% efficiency gain) in standard scenario. Policy: three-tier framework:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Tier 1 ($>$300 requests/day): bidirectional meeting points with full MNL choice model
  \item Tier 2 (100–300 requests/day): single-sided pickup meeting points with door-to-door dropoff
  \item Tier 3 ($<$100 requests/day): door-to-door service
\end{itemize}

\subsection{R5: Deploy Rolling Horizon Re-optimization for Dynamic Demand}
~150 words. Evidence: AblationNoRollingHorizon shows 16.6% increase in vehicle-km and dramatically higher waiting times (avg 3948.8s vs 450.3s). Policy: implement rolling horizon with H≥30 min, Δ=5 min; cloud-based dispatch rather than on-vehicle computation; average decision time <1 second is feasible on standard server hardware.

Closing paragraph (~100 words): Summarize the deployment decision framework. Operators should assess walking tolerance, fleet size, and demand density before choosing service mode. The three-tier framework provides a practical decision tool for Chinese city DRT planners.
