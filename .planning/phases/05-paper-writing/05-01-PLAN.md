Required subsection structure (use \paragraph or prose flow, not \subsection):

Paragraph 1 — Motivation (~150 words):
DRT as a flexible transit mode for low-density urban areas in China; challenge of balancing operational efficiency (vehicle-km) with service accessibility; door-to-door DRT is costly; meeting points reduce detours but impose walking burden on passengers.

Paragraph 2 — Literature context and gap (~200 words):
Introduce DARP/DRT literature briefly. State that Cortenbach et al. (2024, TR Part C) introduced DARPmp with single-sided meeting points (pickup flexible, dropoff fixed) using Tabu Search, but did not model passenger choice. Wu et al. (2025, TR Part E) proposed rolling horizon DRT but without meeting points. No existing work combines: (a) bidirectional meeting point sets (M_r^P for pickup, M_r^D for dropoff), (b) explicit MNL passenger choice with outside option, and (c) rolling horizon re-optimization in a many-to-many DRT setting. This is the gap this paper fills.

Paragraph 3 — This paper (~200 words):
We propose a many-to-many DRT model with bidirectional meeting point sets M_r^P and M_r^D, where the system jointly optimizes pickup and dropoff meeting point assignment while accounting for passenger choice via a Multinomial Logit (MNL) model with three passenger types (price-sensitive, time-sensitive, walk-sensitive) and an outside option. The model is formulated as a three-layer coupled system: (1) service offer generation, (2) passenger response, (3) dynamic dispatch with rolling horizon ALNS re-optimization. We solve small instances exactly (MILP/Gurobi) and large instances with a rolling horizon ALNS heuristic.

Paragraph 4 — Contributions (use \begin{enumerate}...\end{enumerate}):
List all 4 contributions verbatim from CONTEXT.md:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item First formulation of many-to-many DRT with bidirectional meeting point sets ($M_r^P$, $M_r^D$) and MNL passenger choice as a coupled three-layer model.
  \item MILP exact benchmark and rolling horizon ALNS heuristic for the bidirectional DARP with passenger choice.
  \item Empirical demonstration of a $-34.9\%$ vehicle efficiency gain (vkm per accepted trip: 2383.85 vs.\ 3662.33) over the door-to-door baseline.
  \item Equity analysis across three passenger types (Gini coefficient = 0.1216) and five actionable policy recommendations for Chinese city DRT operators.
\end{enumerate}

Paragraph 5 — Paper organization (~100 words):
"The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Section~\ref{sec:literature} reviews related work. Section~\ref{sec:model} presents the problem formulation. Section~\ref{sec:algorithm} describes the solution methodology. Section~\ref{sec:experiments} reports numerical experiments. Section~\ref{sec:policy} discusses policy implications. Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} concludes."

Use \label{sec:intro} on the \section command.
