\subsection{Dial-a-Ride and Demand-Responsive Transit}
~300 words. Cover: DARP as a variant of VRP with time windows and ride time constraints (Cordeau & Laporte 2007 \cite{cordeau2007}); growth of DRT in low-density areas; recent DRT surveys (Ho et al. 2018 \cite{ho2018}); Molenbruch et al. (2017) \cite{molenbruch2017} on flexible transport. Note that classical DARP assumes door-to-door service; this paper relaxes that assumption.

\subsection{Meeting Points and Virtual Stops}
~300 words. Cover: Stiglic et al. (2015 \cite{stiglic2015}, 2018 \cite{stiglic2018}) introduced virtual stops in ridesharing to reduce detours; Cortenbach et al. (2024) \cite{cortenbach2024} extended to DARPmp with single-sided meeting points (pickup flexible, dropoff fixed), solved with Tabu Search — this is the direct predecessor. Key limitation of Cortenbach et al.: single-sided only (no dropoff flexibility), no passenger choice model, static batch formulation. This paper extends to bidirectional (both M_r^P and M_r^D flexible) with online passenger choice.

\subsection{Passenger Choice in Demand-Responsive Transit}
~250 words. Cover: MNL discrete choice models (Ben-Akiva & Lerman 1985 \cite{benakiva1985}); application of discrete choice to ridesharing and DRT (Lavieri & Bhat 2019 \cite{lavieri2019}); passenger heterogeneity in transit mode choice; outside option modeling. Note that most DRT optimization papers treat acceptance as deterministic (system assigns, passenger complies); this paper models stochastic acceptance via MNL with outside option. Self-cite Work 1 \cite{jin2024work1} for the MNL framework in many-to-one DRT.

\subsection{Dynamic Scheduling and Rolling Horizon Methods}
~250 words. Cover: online DARP and dynamic insertion heuristics; Bent & Van Hentenryck (2004) \cite{bent2004} on rolling horizon for VRP; Ropke & Pisinger (2006) \cite{ropke2006} ALNS as the basis for the repair operator; Wu et al. (2025) \cite{wu2025} rolling horizon DRT — direct predecessor for the algorithm design. Key limitation of Wu et al.: no meeting points, no passenger choice. This paper combines rolling horizon with bidirectional MP assignment and MNL choice.

\subsection{Research Gap and Positioning}
~100 words. Synthesis paragraph: "Table~\ref{tab:literature-gap} summarizes the positioning of this paper relative to key prior works." Then include a LaTeX table:

\end{tabular}
\end{table}

Close with: "This paper is the first to jointly address all four dimensions."
