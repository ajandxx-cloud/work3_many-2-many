\subsection{Coverage--Efficiency Pareto Frontier}
\label{sec:pareto}
% -----------------------------------------------------------------------

To isolate the efficiency contribution of bidirectional meeting point assignment
from coverage effects, we sweep the rejection penalty $\Gamma \in
\{0, 5, 10, 20, 50, 100\}$ and record the resulting (served share, vkm per
served trip, social welfare $W$) triple for FullModel at scale 200 (3 seeds).
A higher $\Gamma$ penalises unserved passengers more heavily, incentivising the
model to accept a larger share of requests at the cost of higher per-trip vkm.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The frontier confirms that the relationship between coverage and efficiency is
not a simple inverse: as $\Gamma$ rises from 0 to 100, served share increases
but vkm per served trip also rises, indicating that the additional (marginal)
trips served at higher $\Gamma$ are inherently less efficient~---~longer routes,
less spatial clustering. This is the expected behaviour of a well-calibrated
choice model: the passengers who opt out at low $\Gamma$ are precisely those
whose trips would be routed inefficiently. The social welfare metric $W$ peaks
at an intermediate $\Gamma$, balancing passenger coverage against per-trip cost.
Operators can use this frontier to select $\Gamma$ according to their service
mandate: a coverage-focused operator selects high $\Gamma$; an
efficiency-focused operator selects $\Gamma = 0$.
