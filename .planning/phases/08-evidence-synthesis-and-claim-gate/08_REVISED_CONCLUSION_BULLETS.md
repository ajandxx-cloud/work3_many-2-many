# Phase 8 Revised Conclusion Bullets

**Phase:** 08 - Evidence Synthesis and Claim Gate
**Date:** 2026-06-16
**Status:** complete

These are conclusion claim units for Phase 9 refresh, not a complete conclusion.

## Allowed Conclusion Claim Units

- The paper's supported contribution is an integrated, choice-aware,
  evidence-gated simulation framework for bidirectional pickup/dropoff
  meeting-point service design in many-to-many DRT.
- The main formal synthetic evidence supports a conditional efficiency claim:
  BidirectionalMP/FullModel has lower vehicle-km intensity than the tested
  door-to-door and single-sided choice baselines under paired synthetic
  experiments.
- That efficiency finding must be concluded together with the main trade-off:
  FullModel serves a smaller share, so vehicle-km, served share, behavioral
  acceptance, choice rejection, and feasibility rejection must be read jointly.
- Matched-coverage controls show that completed pairs remain consistent with
  a FullModel efficiency advantage, but 15 durable failed FullModel matched
  rows limit the strength of equal-coverage interpretation.
- Fixed accepted-set diagnostics support a routing/service-design signal on
  vkm per served request only; they do not establish unconditional
  vkm/original dominance or a behavioral headline claim.
- Utility sensitivity, walking-radius/meeting-point-density, and fleet-demand
  stress results should be summarized as reduced robustness diagnostics, not
  universal parameter rules.
- Equity outputs may be concluded as modeled type-level and individual-burden
  diagnostics, with equity claim grade exploratory because passenger types are
  simulation-range constructs.
- Algorithm diagnostics may be concluded as implementation and diagnostic-scope
  support, not ALNS optimality or deployment readiness.
- The Beijing-inspired material is synthetic and illustrative only; it cannot
  validate real Beijing operations or direct citywide policy recommendations.
- Future work must prioritize real or semi-real OD, road-network, meeting-point,
  request-time, passenger-preference, and fleet calibration/validation.

## Forbidden Conclusion Content

- Do not repeat legacy 29.1% or 35.0% superiority claims.
- Do not claim first/only novelty.
- Do not state that bidirectional meeting points dominate under all metrics.
- Do not present gamma as a Pareto frontier.
- Do not claim strong equity benefits.
- Do not state that Beijing results validate real operations.
- Do not give deployment-ready or regulator-prescription language.
