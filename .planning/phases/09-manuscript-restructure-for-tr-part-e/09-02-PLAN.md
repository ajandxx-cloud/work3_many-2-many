# Plan 09-02: Claim-Gated Abstract, Keywords, and Highlights Plan

<objective>
Create a revised abstract planning artifact that replaces the current overstrong
abstract with a TR-E-compatible, conditional, claim-gated abstract template plus
keywords and required Elsevier highlights.
</objective>

<must_haves>
- D-03: Title and abstract use cautious, conditional language.
- D-19: Abstract opens with the fair-evidence problem.
- D-20: Core contribution sentence frames an evidence-chain framework.
- D-23: Abstract closes with conditional design guidance and boundary conditions.
</must_haves>

<truths>
- The current abstract states old numerical results and policy implications before Phase 8 claim grading exists.
- TR-E abstract guidance requires a concise factual abstract no longer than 250 words.
- Elsevier highlights are required and should be 3 to 5 bullets, each no more than 85 characters.
- Phase 8 supported/unsupported claim artifacts are missing in this workspace.
</truths>

<threat_model>
| Threat | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Unsupported numbers enter the revised abstract | high | Use claim placeholders keyed to Phase 8 artifact paths |
| Abstract repeats unconditional superiority language | high | Add forbidden wording and replacement wording tables |
| Highlights become unsupported claims | medium | Require each highlight to cite a supported-claim placeholder |
| AI-assisted writing declaration is forgotten at submission | low | Add a final-submission note about Elsevier AI declaration policy |
</threat_model>
