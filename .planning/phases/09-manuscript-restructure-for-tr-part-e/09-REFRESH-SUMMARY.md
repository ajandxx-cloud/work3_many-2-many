## 7. Abstract Claim Boundary

The abstract may state the integrated framework contribution and the conditional
formal synthetic vehicle-km intensity result only when paired with served share,
acceptance/rejection, coverage-control, diagnostic, and synthetic-data
boundaries. It must not include legacy effect sizes, universal superiority,
real Beijing validation, first/only novelty, deployment readiness, or strong
equity claims.

## 8. Introduction Contribution Boundary

The contribution list is limited to:

- choice-aware dynamic service-design simulation framework;
- consistent passenger-response baseline taxonomy;
- paired formal synthetic evidence with denominator discipline;
- coverage-confounding controls;
- bounded robustness/equity/algorithm/case diagnostics;
- reproducibility-oriented Phase 6 evidence package, with final regeneration
  still pending in Phase 10.

## 9. Experiment Section Evidence-Family Boundary

The experiment plan separates:

- main behavioral evidence;
- coverage-control evidence;
- fixed accepted-set routing diagnostic;
- robustness diagnostics;
- equity/type-level diagnostics;
- Beijing-inspired synthetic illustration;
- reproducibility and failure-row reporting.

Only the main behavioral evidence may support the abstract efficiency claim, and
only with coverage/rejection context.

## 10. Table/Figure Boundary

The display plan requires method taxonomy, metric denominators, main behavioral
results with served share and rejection, matched coverage, fixed accepted-set
diagnostics, robustness, equity, claim-evidence matrix, evidence pipeline, and
framework diagrams. Diagnostic and Beijing-inspired displays must not be shown
as headline or real-case validation.

## 11. Managerial Insight Boundary

Managerial insights are simulation-based and conditional. They may say that
meeting-point flexibility can reduce vehicle-km intensity in tested synthetic
paired experiments and that operators should evaluate efficiency with coverage
and rejection. They may not recommend immediate deployment, real Beijing policy,
universal superiority, equity guarantees, or citywide cost reductions.
