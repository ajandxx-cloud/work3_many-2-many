# TR-E Positioning Lock

## Purpose

This lock defines the manuscript framing that Phase 3 must follow before any
prose edits begin.

This is a TR-E logistics and operations positioning artifact. It locks allowed
operational service-design evidence, journal-fit rationale, and safe mechanism
wording.

This is not a manuscript rewrite, numerical claim replacement, evidence rerun,
policy validation, deployable decision-tool claim, or optimization-method
supremacy claim.

## Prohibited Framing

Do not state or imply any of the following:

- the paper's primary contribution is policy validation or a TR Part A-style
  policy intervention;
- the framework is a deployable policy or operations decision tool;
- FullModel universally dominates DoorToDoor, all baselines, or all service
  conditions;
- the current evidence is real-world Beijing validation or an empirical Beijing
  case study;
- Gamma controls routing, offer generation, acceptance behavior, policy, or an
  endogenous Pareto frontier;
- the model performs `co-optimization of meeting points, routing, and passenger response`;
- route construction endogenously optimizes over passenger acceptance
  probabilities;
- matched-coverage, fixed-accepted-set, robustness, equity/type, Gamma/Pareto,
  or MILP outputs are the primary headline estimate;
- MILP is a full exact dynamic benchmark or that ALNS has been proven
  near-optimal;
- any final percentage, confidence interval, significance statement, table
  number, or figure number is locked before Phase 4 provenance checks.

## Core Contribution Lock

The safe core sentence is:

Passenger-response-aware bidirectional meeting-point consolidation can reduce routing intensity per served trip under tested synthetic service-design conditions, while creating measurable coverage and passenger-type trade-offs.

Allowed contribution structure:

- operational problem: many-to-many DRT service design with pickup and dropoff
  meeting-point consolidation;
- mechanism: a passenger-response-aware simulation framework with binary-logit
  offer acceptance;
- routing context: rolling-horizon routing and heuristic insertion/ALNS
  operations;
- evidence posture: conditional service-design evidence from formal Phase 6
  outputs, with diagnostics and limitations labeled separately;
- trade-off posture: lower routing intensity claims must travel with served
  share, coverage, acceptance, feasibility, and passenger-type trade-offs.

This contribution lock does not authorize final numerical wording. Phase 3 may
use non-numeric phrasing and placeholders only where final values depend on
Phase 4 provenance.

## Journal-Fit Rationale

TR-E fit should be argued through logistics and operations management:

- DRT/DARP operations and on-demand mobility service design;
- meeting-point service consolidation for many-to-many passenger flows;
- dynamic routing and rolling-horizon fleet operations;
- operator-facing routing intensity, served-share, coverage, and passenger
  response trade-offs;
- simulation and operations-research evidence that clarifies service-design
  conditions rather than claiming policy validation.

The paper may discuss public-service implications only as bounded consequences
of operational service design. Public-service language must not replace the
logistics/operations journal-fit rationale.

## Do Not Before Gate Rules

- Do not edit manuscript prose before Phase 2 creates the positioning lock,
  claim ledger, and blockers/safe-claims table.
- Do not inject final percentages, improvement values, confidence intervals,
  significance language, table numbers, or figure numbers before Phase 4
  provenance checks.
- Do not promote diagnostic evidence into the primary behavioral headline.
- Do not use root legacy CSVs, smoke outputs, archive outputs, pilot outputs,
  or ad hoc outputs as formal manuscript evidence unless a later phase
  explicitly audits and labels them.
- Do not describe Gamma as endogenous behavior unless future model work
  implements and validates that behavior.
- Do not describe the Beijing scenario as real-world validation unless future
  evidence work implements reproducible public-data ingestion.
- Do not describe MILP as a full exact dynamic benchmark or ALNS
  near-optimality proof.
- Do not use the `TR-E submission-ready` label before Phase 5 hard readiness
  gates pass.

## Readiness Label Boundary

`TR-E submission-ready` is not allowed before Phase 5 hard gates pass.

the following are true or documented with no submission impact:

- manuscript compilation passes;
- formal validation passes;
- targeted tests pass;
- numerical claim-ledger coverage is complete;
- all manuscript tables and figures trace to `results/formal/phase06/`;
- prohibited wording scans are clean.

If any hard gate fails or remains incomplete, the readiness label must be one
of:

- `TR-E near-ready with minor blockers`;
- `not ready due to specific blockers`.
